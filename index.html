German Renewable Energy Analysis 2022-2025

Exploring the relationship between weather conditions and electricity generation from wind and solar sources

📊 Project Overview

Research Questions

❓ Question 1

Weather Impact on Renewables

How strongly do weather conditions (especially wind and sunshine) explain daily electricity generation from wind (onshore/offshore) and PV in Germany?

❓ Question 2

Generation Mix Shifts

How does Germany's generation mix shift on days with high renewable output — and which conventional sources are reduced the most?

❓ Question 3

Forecast Accuracy

How accurate are SMARD day-ahead forecasts for wind and PV compared to actual generation — and does error vary by season?

📈 Data Sources

  • SMARD API: German electricity market data (2022-2025)
    → https://www.smard.de/
  • Open-Meteo API: Historical weather data for major German cities
    → https://open-meteo.com/
  • Time Range: January 1, 2022 - December 31, 2025
    Regions Covered: 50Hertz, Amprion, TenneT, TransnetBW (TSO control areas)

🔬 Methodology

  • Data aggregated from SMARD electricity market API and Open-Meteo weather API
  • R² analysis to measure weather's explanatory power
  • Generation mix comparison on high vs low renewable days
  • Seasonal forecast accuracy evaluation (MAE, RMSE, MAPE)
  • Regional TSO analysis across Germany

ℹ️ About This Project

This project analyzes the German electricity market and its relationship with weather conditions, focusing on renewable energy generation (wind and solar) from 2022 to 2025.
