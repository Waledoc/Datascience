Weather, Holidays and Electricity in Germany

This website presents our project on how weather conditions and public holidays influence electricity consumption, electricity generation and electricity prices in Germany between 2022 and 2025.

Team: Marcel Kawski, Waleed Mahmood and Leon Krückmeyer

View Main Findings Explore Research Questions Go to API Page

Overview of the four German TSO zones: 50Hertz, Amprion, TenneT and TransnetBW.

Project Question

To what extent do weather conditions and public holidays influence electricity consumption and generation in Germany?

Main Findings

These three findings summarize the most important results of the project.

Main Finding 1: Holidays and Consumption

Electricity consumption is lowest on holiday-period days. Regular working days show higher average consumption, and Sundays are also lower than regular days, although the strongest reduction appears during holiday periods.

Open Main Finding 1

Main Finding 2: Weather and Generation

Weather has a clear influence on renewable electricity generation. Higher wind speeds are linked to higher wind generation, while warmer and sunnier conditions are linked to higher solar generation.

Open Main Finding 2

Main Finding 3: Regional Differences across TSO Zones

Clear differences appear across the four TSO zones. Amprion and TenneT show higher average consumption, and regional patterns reflect industry, population and generation mix.

Open Main Finding 3

APIs and Data Sources

The project combines electricity, weather and holiday data at daily level.

SMARD

Electricity consumption and electricity generation data for Germany and the TSO regions.

Open-Meteo

Daily weather data such as wind speed, maximum temperature and precipitation.

OpenHolidays

Holiday data for German federal states, transformed into daily holiday observations.
